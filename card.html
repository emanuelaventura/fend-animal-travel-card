Sun Parakeet or Sun Conure

Sun conures enjoy listening to music, to which they occasionally sing and dance.

  • Scientific name:
    Aratinga solstitialis
  • Origin:
    northeastern South America
  • Aspect:
    predominantly golden-yellow plumage and orange-flushed
    underparts and face
  • Behaviour:
    they are very social birds, typically living in flocks.
    They form monogamous pairs for reproduction, and nest in palm cavities in the tropics
  • Diet: fruits, flowers, berries, blossoms, seeds, nuts, and insects.

The sun conure is a medium-sized, vibrantly colored parrot. It is noted for its very loud squawks and screams and is popular as pet because of its
bright coloration and curious nature.
Due to their inquisitive temperaments, they demand a great deal of attention from their owners, with whom they can be loving and cuddly.
Hand-reared pets can be very friendly towards people with whom they are familiar, but they may be aggressive with strangers and even territorial with visitors.
Sun conures are capable of learning many tricks and can even perform in front of a live audience.
In captivity, their lifespans range from 15 to 30 years.
They are now listed as endangered by the IUCN.
